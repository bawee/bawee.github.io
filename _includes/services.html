Research

My research focus

Microbiology

I study a diverse range of pathogenic bacteria and investigate how they can exchange genetic material to speed up the evolution of traits such as virulence and antibiotic resistance. I have researched multi-drug resistant Escherichia coli, Legionella, Staphylococci, Chlamydia and Pseudomonas in addition to the microbiome, which are complex communities of bacteria.

Genomics

I apply genome sequencing to perform compartive analyses on large datasets of bacterial isolates. These large datasets are generated using short-read Illumina technology or long-read Nanopore sequencing.

Epidemiology

Genomics provides the highest level of resolution in determining the relationships of cases in an outbreak. We can also investigate the spread of genetic material between bacteria, revealing underlying genetic exchange communities.
